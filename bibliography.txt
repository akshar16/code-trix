Source 1:
Name: Our World in Data – CO₂ and Greenhouse Gas Emissions Dataset
Link: https://github.com/owid/co2-data
Description: 
This dataset includes global data on carbon dioxide emissions, GDP, population, and energy consumption from 1750 onwards, collected and maintained 
by researchers at Our World in Data.

Source 2:
Name: Berkeley Earth – Global Land Temperatures by Country
Link: https://berkeleyearth.org/data/
Description: 
Provides historical temperature data by country, dating back to 1743, compiled by climate scientists at the Berkeley Earth Surface Temperature Project.

Source 3:
Name: Kaggle – Global Land Temperatures by Country (Adapted version)
Link: https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/vijayvvenkitesh/global-land-temperatures-by-country
Description: 
A Kaggle-hosted version of the Berkeley Earth dataset, restructured for ease of use in data science and machine learning projects.

Source 4:
Name: Carbon Intensity Estimates (Based on IPCC Global Averages)
Link: https://www.ipcc.ch/report/ar6/wg3/
Description: 
Approximate global average carbon emissions (in gCO₂/kWh) for various energy sources such as coal, oil, natural gas, solar, wind, and nuclear,
as reported by the Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change (IPCC) in their Sixth Assessment Report (AR6).
